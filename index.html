About Me

I am Md. Habib Talukder, a recent graduate in Apparel Manufacturing and Technology from BGMEA University of Fashion & Technology (BUFT), where I ranked 6th in my class with a CGPA of 3.31/4.00. My research interests lie in Sustainable Supply Chain, Smart Textile, and Waste Management.

My research interests lie at the intersection of sustainability, smart textiles, and supply chain optimization. I am particularly focused on circular economy practices, waste management in the fashion industry, and integrating cutting-edge technology to revolutionize traditional apparel manufacturing processes.

Beyond academics, I have actively contributed to the apparel community through leadership roles, research initiatives, and public speaking competitions. I believe in the power of innovation and collaboration to create a more sustainable and efficient future for the global fashion industry.

Publications And Research Experience

On the Integration of Sustainable Fabric Production Techniques in Bangladesh's Apparel Sector

2024

Sustainable Manufacturing, Textile Production Published in Textile and Fashion Research Journal

Co-author: Takeira U. M.

This research investigates the integration of sustainable fabric production techniques in Bangladesh's rapidly growing apparel sector. The study analyzes eco-friendly dyeing and washing methods, their environmental impact, and potential for large-scale implementation in local manufacturing facilities.

Published in: Textile and Fashion Research Journal, Vol. 15(3), pp. 120-132

Reducing Fabric Waste through Digital Pattern Cutting: A Case Study

2023

Waste Management, Digital Technology, Pattern Design

This case study explores how digital pattern cutting technology can significantly reduce fabric waste in apparel manufacturing. Through analysis of traditional vs. digital cutting methods, the research demonstrates measurable waste reduction and efficiency improvements in the production process.

Published in: BUFT Student Research Bulletin, Issue 8, pp. 45-51
